My name is Matthew Bender, and I am a sophomore Computer Engineering student at the University of Maryland, College Park.

This past summer I had an internship at ViaSat where I wrote a combination of frontend and backend code for clients to use. There I also participated in the ViaSat hackathon, where myself along with 2 other interns teamed up to code for 24 straight hours. At the end, we ended up winning country-wide for our company, with a web-based mapping system of ViaSat's offices that offered searching by office or room number, as well as showing the quickest route from one office to the next.

At Maryland so far, I have taken classes on Data Stauctures, Computer Systems, Programming Languages, Algorithms, Digital Logic Design, Digital Circuits, Calculus, and Discrete Mathematics. I also took place in the Windward Code Wars my freshman year, on a team with 4 other freshman. The goal was to design an A.I. that would compete with rival teams' A.I.'s to travel a course and reach goals that were constantly changing. Our team, "String theory;" placed 1st at the University of Maryland and went on to win 2nd place in the worldwide competition.
